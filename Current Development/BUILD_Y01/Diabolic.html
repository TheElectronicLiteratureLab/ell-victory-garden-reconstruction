Every time I hear this story, the Kiosk says, whether it's told by a rabbi, a priest, a minister, or a doctor of philosophy, it comes out with the same happy ending. The spirit returns, the monks are saved. How inspiring, how full of hope.

How sad and unreal.

Because I know what I'm talking about — I've been to those deep woods, I've seen the weathered scraps of the rabbi's hut and the broken walls of the old monastery. And that's not all I've seen.

Buried in the old kitchen midden I found the skeletons of three old men, each showing the same distinctive marks. The ribcages had been pierced, the wrists and ankles shattered.

What can this mean?
