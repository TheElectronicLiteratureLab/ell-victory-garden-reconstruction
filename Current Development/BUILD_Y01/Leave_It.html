Leave It

Urquhart squinted at the grackle as it pecked breakfast. "Temporarily. At least they said temporarily."

Harley snickered. "So I told them I could be temporarily unemployed. Fitzmaurice said they'd call it 'stress leave.'"

Urquhart tried to shoo the bird away. It made a noise like a broken bus transmission and would not stir. "In your case," he pointed out, "'stress leave' is an oxymoron."

"I've been having strange dreams," he said. He was silent for a while, rubbing his brow.

"Oh well," Urquhart answered, "that explains it."
