Well In Hand

All the interest seemed to be down at the shallow end of the swimming hole, the end adjoining the property slated for development. A small knot of protesters had climbed over a fence down there, a few dozen at most, wearing telegenic T-shirts and bandanas and carrying signs that read "Up the Creek," "Eat My Nine Iron," and "Whitman Had The Right Idea." A couple of Parks Department people were on the scene but at the moment they weren't interfering. They seemed caught up in the spectacle.

And indeed there was a spectacle here, something rare and tasty to shoot for the dinner table. It wasn't the protesters that the flying eye wanted — it was that figure standing out in front of them, the fellow with the moustache, sunglasses, and heavily cleft chin, the guy in the strange military drag, the one now stepping to the water's edge and reaching for his—

"Oh Jesus they won't showthat Live At Five," Macarthur predicted.
