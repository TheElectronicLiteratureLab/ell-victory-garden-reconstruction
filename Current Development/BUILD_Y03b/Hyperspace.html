Hyperspace

Constructive hypertexts require a capability to act: to create, to change, and to recover particular encounters within the developing body of knowledge. These encounters... are versions of what they are becoming, a structure for what does not yet exist. ... A constructive hypertext should be a tool for inventing, discovering, viewing, and testing multiple, alternative, organizational structures....

— Michael Joyce, "Siren Shapes"

There is no simple way to say this.

— Joyce, afternoon
