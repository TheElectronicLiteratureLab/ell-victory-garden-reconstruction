Like 'progressive' and 'liberal' in years gone by, the term 'conservative' had taken on many meanings, some of them schismatically opposed. Tate's conservatism was of the quietistic, risk-averse kind. It was, Heidel thought, a coward's conservatism, a belief in always fighting another day.
