Safe At Last

They were turned more or less broadside across the road. Harley looked out his window and saw the two chase cars brake in smartly, swinging around to close the box. Spotlights swivelled in on them, one of them catching Harley right in the eyes. He started to put his hand up against the glare but remembered what he'd learned about soldiers, guerillas, terrorists, and other excitable boys. No sudden movements. One of the searchlights glinted off the rearview mirror and Harley heard somebody ratchet his shotgun.

"For God's sake sit still," he told Urquhart. "Don't even fart."

All it will take, he realized, is for one of them to see what he thinks is a gun barrel or a muzzle flash, and when they get finished we'll look like Bonnie and Clyde. Shit, they'll say we were Bonnie and Clyde. Or Willie Horton and... he glanced at Urquhart's costume. Oh perfect.
