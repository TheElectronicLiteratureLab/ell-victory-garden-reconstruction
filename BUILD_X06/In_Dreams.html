In Dreams

She wakes me later that night, or actually in the small hours of the morning. It's happened before. She's been dreaming. I roll over to the nightstand and pour us a drink. We share the glass.

"It was a wedding," she tells me. "Only it took place in a huge auditorium, on this gigantic stage. I want to call it a 'sound stage.' Everyone was wearing costumes from some opera. Aida or something like that. Only I've never seen Aida. "

I stroke her temple. "It's culture," I explain. "You don't have to have seen it."

"The groom was Harley." She smiled. "You should have seen him. He was beautiful. And... the father of the bride was... oh Jesus it was Boris Urquhart."

"Which means the bride wasn't Veronica," I noted. "She must have been Emily."
