Pragmatics

"The problem with all this," Tate mused, "is that I don't believe in coincidence."

Heidel shrugged, standing in the darkness. What Tate might or might not believe mattered little to him. Tate was after all a pragmatist: what mattered above all was what he would choose to do.

"I don't care how the war came about," Heidel called up to him. "It's here now. It defines the conditions with which we have to work."

"What?" Tate said, clearly distracted.

"The war," Heidel repeated, beginning to grow peevish.

"Oh yes," said Tate. "There's the war, yes."
