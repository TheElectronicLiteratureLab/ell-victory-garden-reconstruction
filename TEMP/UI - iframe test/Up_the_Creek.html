Up the Creek

The four of them climbed reluctantly out of the water and parked themselves under an old sycamore. "Golf courses aren't what you'd call a regular geologic feature," Miles explained to Harley.

"Right," Thea joined in. "To keep one going in this part of the world you need three things: cheap labor, plenty of water, and a few tons of chemical fertilizer."

"Guess where it all goes when it runs off?" Veronica said, tugging his earlobes.

"Ah."

"Actually, runoff from the construction alone would probably devastate this here swimming hole," Miles estimated. "What washes off the back nine would pretty much ensure it stayed that way. To say nothing of effluent from the sewer or septic systems they'd need for the condos."
