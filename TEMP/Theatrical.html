Theatrical

"Execute Lights," Urquhart said. The overhead lamps dimmed smoothly as the room heard and obeyed. Urquhart laid his cards on the table and fanned them. "Screens," he called. Nothing happened.

"Screens?" he tried again. Still negative function. "Screens pleece," he said finally, falling back on the Boris Badenov voice which this software seemed to prefer.

Drive motors hummed and at either end of the room the whiteboards rolled back and the big displays behind them ticked to life.
