Thirsty

"Any of that for me?" Leroy asked, pantomiming a grab for the whisky.

"You can't even drive yet," Thea reminded him, tucking the bottle under her arm. "And you're the Just Say No generation, remember?" She reached up, hooked her fingers through his Harpo Marx tangles, and hauled him into the living room.

"Do as we say, right?" Leroy replied, shaking back his hair. "After you guys've had all the fun."

Thea set the bottle down a safe distance from her thirsty son, hit the lights and dropped onto the sofa. "You might find a Corona in the fridge," she conceded. "As in one and only one."
